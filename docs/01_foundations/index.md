# 01 · Foundations — The Foundational Debugger

---

## ◉ About This Section

This section is for programmers who are new to debugging — or who have been debugging by guessing and want to build a proper foundation.

The goal here is not speed. It is **literacy**: learning to read what the system is telling you, understanding what kind of error you are facing, and following a repeatable process before asking for help.

---

## ◉ Who Should Read This Section

- Students writing their first programs
- Anyone who has been ignoring error messages and guessing at fixes
- Anyone who reaches for a screenshot before reading the error
- High school (Grade 10–12) and first-year undergraduate students

## ◉ Assumed Prior Knowledge

None. This section assumes you are starting from the beginning.

---

## ◉ What This Section Does Not Cover

This section does not teach you how to use a debugger tool, how to investigate complex multi-file bugs, or how to debug framework-driven systems. Those belong in [02 · Investigation](../02_investigation/index.md) and beyond.

---

**If you cannot identify the error type, location, and message — you are not debugging yet.**
